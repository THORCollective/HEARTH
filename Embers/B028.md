## Why
- Bidirectional C2 over a legitimate web service is a documented Turla tradecraft pattern and the defining novelty of STOCKSTAY — Mandiant explicitly calls out Render/Glitch WSS abuse and a `/ws` server handler, making it the single most distinctive and huntable behavior in the report.
- The behavior is durable across campaigns: filenames, lures (Ukrainian military/drone themes), and even the specific C2 domain change, but "non-browser .NET process -> WSS -> free PaaS `/ws`" persists from Jan-2024 through Nov-2025.
- It is a true Embers hunt — the abused channel (Render/Glitch/HTTPS-443) is normal in most enterprises, so detection requires baselining benign usage first rather than alerting on the protocol itself.
- It is cheaply observable with telemetry most shops already have (Sysmon EID 22/3, Defender DeviceNetworkEvents, proxy SNI + HTTP 101 Upgrade) and pivots cleanly into high-confidence STOCKSTAY confirmers (Run-key/Startup persistence, websocket-sharp, WM_COPYDATA, the provided YARA).
