WINDOWS DETECTION: (1) Process telemetry — Sysmon EID 1 / Defender DeviceProcessEvents for: wmic.exe `/Namespace:\\root\SecurityCenter2 Path AntiVirusProduct Get displayName`, powershell Get-WmiObject/Get-CimInstance with `root\SecurityCenter2` + class `AntiVirusProduct`, `reg query` of Defender/EDR keys, `sc query`, `tasklist`, `Get-Process | Where-Object` filtering for security-tool process names, `netsh advfirewall` queries. (3) Defender KQL: `DeviceProcessEvents | where ProcessCommandLine has_any("SecurityCenter2","AntiVirusProduct","windefend","MsMpEng","sense","cylancesvc","csagent","sentinelagent") or FileName in~ ("wmic.exe","sc.exe","tasklist.exe")` and `DeviceRegistryEvents` for access to Defender/Uninstall keys. COLLECTION FIELDS (capture per event): timestamp, DeviceName, AccountName, AccountSid, parent process (FileName+CommandLine+path+hash), child/initiating process (FileName+CommandLine+path+hash+signer), registry key/value accessed (EID 4663 ObjectName), WMI namespace+class, process integrity level, logon session ID, and parent-process directory (flag user-writable paths %TEMP%/%APPDATA%/C:\Users\<user>\<random>\). COLLECTION WINDOW: 30 days, to establish which (process, security-key) pairs are normal. DELIVERABLE FORMAT (allowlist): produce an allowlist of approved security-software-discovery sources — e.g. {SCCM/MECM inventory, Tanium, Lansweeper, Nessus/Qualys agents, PDQ, the EDR/AV product processes themselves, named admin scripts} keyed by process name + signer + path — and triage everything OUTSIDE the allowlist. ## Why
- ESET and Microsoft both document Amadey enumerating installed antivirus and reporting it in the C2 handshake (`av:` fingerprint field) to decide whether to fully deploy — making security-software discovery a reliable, early pre-infection tell across the whole Amadey/StealC delivery chain.
- The behavior overlaps heavily with legitimate admin/inventory activity, which is exactly why it belongs in Embers: the value is a 30-day baseline and allowlist, not a single noisy alert, letting hunters isolate the automated/unsigned/user-path enumeration that admins never produce.
- It is a natural sensor for the entire intrusion: catching the recon stage gives defenders time to act before credential theft, screenshotting, RDP enablement, and local-admin persistence occur.
- Defender already ships Threat Analytics profiles for Amadey and StealC and behavioral signatures (`Behavior:Win64/Stealc`); a discovery baseline complements those by catching pre-detonation reconnaissance the AV signatures may miss.
