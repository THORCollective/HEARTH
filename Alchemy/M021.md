## Why
- The malware bundles a renamed portable Tor binary (`ugate.exe`) and pushes stolen BIP39 seed phrases, Ethereum/Bitcoin-WIF private keys, and screenshots out over an onion-routed (layered asymmetric-encrypted) channel via `curl --socks5-hostname localhost:9050`, separate from a normal HTTPS C2 — the defining shape of T1048.002 exfiltration over an asymmetric-encrypted non-C2 protocol.
- Static defenses fail here: onion C2 addresses (`...jlglamisad.onion`) and sample hashes rotate, and DNS resolves *inside* Tor so destination domains are invisible at the resolver — only a behavioral/volumetric model of the encrypted egress generalizes across infrastructure churn.
- Tor egress blends with rare-but-legitimate use, so a flat block/match is both noisy and lossy; scoring NEW-or-rare process + egress to live Tor entry/relay nodes + low-jitter beaconing (article: ~500ms clipboard loop, continuous C2 poll) + per-host byte-volume anomaly isolates malicious exfil from the legitimate Tor background.
- This pairs with the endpoint/persistence side of the same intrusion (USB `.lnk` worm propagation, two indefinite `schtasks` XML tasks, `C:\Users\Public\Documents\[5-char]\` staging) — the analytic catches the data leaving even when the dropper variant or onion address is brand new.
