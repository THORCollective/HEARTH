## Why
- The Sliver C2 framework is a powerful tool for adversaries, allowing them to maintain control over compromised systems. It is highly configurable and can be used to evade detection by security tools.
- If this technique is successful, the adversaries would be able to execute commands, exfiltrate data, and move laterally within the network.
- The use of Sliver C2 framework is distinctive of this specific threat, as it was found in an open directory tied to a ransomware affiliate suspected to be connected to the Fog ransomware group.
- The choice of this technique over others mentioned in the CTI report is due to its high actionability, as it leaves clear evidence in logs and telemetry, and its high impact, as it directly enables the adversary's objectives.
