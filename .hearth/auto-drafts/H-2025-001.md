## Why
- Detecting the use of compromised VPN credentials is critical as it represents the initial foothold of an adversary within the network. This initial access can lead to further exploitation and compromise of the network.
- If this technique succeeds, the adversary gains access to the corporate network, potentially bypassing perimeter defenses and gaining a foothold for further actions on objectives.
- This specific implementation ties to larger campaigns as it has been observed in the Fog ransomware affiliate's operations, indicating a potential pattern of behavior that can be used for attribution.
- The use of compromised SonicWall VPN credentials was chosen over other techniques mentioned in the CTI due to its actionability. VPN logs can be analyzed to detect anomalies such as unusual login times, source IP addresses, or user-agent strings, making it a highly observable technique.
