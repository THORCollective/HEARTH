# H207

Gamaredon hard-codes "dead drop resolvers" (DDRs) into its Pterodo-family loaders — the malware first reaches out to attacker-controlled content on legitimate web/paste/social services (Telegra.ph, Teletype, Telegram t.me, Rentry.co, Write.as, Nopaste, Paste.ee, Lesma, dev.to, Mastodon) to retrieve the obfuscated address of the next-stage C2, which is itself often hidden behind a Cloudflare Tunnel (`*.trycloudflare.com`), Cloudflare Worker (`*.workers.dev`), Microsoft Dev Tunnel (`*.devtunnels.ms`), or Loophole. ESET calls the DDR "arguably the most critical component" of the infrastructure because it is the single hard-coded indicator inside the sample. Because the resolver fetch is performed by a Windows scripting interpreter (powershell.exe, wscript.exe, cscript.exe, mshta.exe) rather than a browser, the process-to-destination lineage is a discrete, high-fidelity signal. We hypothesize that on hunted Windows endpoints, an outbound HTTPS/DNS request from a script interpreter (or other non-browser process) to one of these dead-drop platforms — especially one immediately followed by a connection to a tunnel/worker domain — indicates Gamaredon DDR resolution rather than benign user browsing.

## Why
- ESET's 2025 report identifies the hard-coded dead-drop resolver as the most critical and most durable indicator in Gamaredon samples — the actor rotates downstream tunnel/worker C2 constantly, but the DDR fetch is the one behavior every Pterodo loader must perform first, making it the highest-leverage hunt pivot.
- The signal is high-fidelity because of process lineage, not domain reputation: telegra.ph/t.me/rentry.co are legitimately popular, but a script interpreter or Startup-folder HTA resolving them is anomalous and rarely benign, so the hunt avoids the false-positive trap of blocklisting popular platforms.
- It is fully observable on standard Windows telemetry (Sysmon EID 1/3/22, Security 4688, PowerShell 4104, Defender DeviceNetworkEvents/DeviceProcessEvents) — no specialized tooling required — and the DDR→tunnel/worker chain gives a second confirmatory connection to corroborate true positives.
- Mapping the resolver platforms and the downstream `*.trycloudflare.com` / `*.workers.dev` / `*.devtunnels.ms` infrastructure produces reusable detections and a watchlist that survive the actor's frequent C2 rotation, raising long-term cost for Gamaredon and any copycat abusing the same dead-drop pattern.
