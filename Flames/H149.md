# H149

Threat actors run Microsoft 365 AiTM phishing using fake "code of conduct" / "internal regulatory" lures that route victims through Cloudflare-CAPTCHA gated staging pages (e.g., compliance-protectionoutlook[.]de) before a final Microsoft sign-in proxy — yielding stolen session tokens that re-enter Entra ID with anomalous-token, unfamiliar-properties, or impossible-travel risk signals.

## Why
- This campaign hit ~35,000 users across 13,000 organizations in 26 countries (92% US-based) per Microsoft's May 2026 disclosure — Healthcare, Financial Services, and Professional Services are the top targets, all sectors with regulatory-themed lure susceptibility
- AiTM token theft renders MFA ineffective — once the session cookie is stolen, the attacker bypasses MFA entirely on every subsequent service hit; detecting at the click-through-to-anomalous-sign-in window is the last reliable layer before mailbox compromise
- The Cloudflare CAPTCHA + image-selection CAPTCHA double-gate is specifically designed to defeat sandbox detonation and URL-rewrite scanning — defenders cannot rely on inline URL inspection, only on post-click identity telemetry
- The "code of conduct" / regulatory-compliance lure theme is novel and effective against compliance-aware industries (healthcare, financial services) — it is likely to recur across multiple operator clusters in 2026, and a generalized hunt on the click→risk-event sequence will catch follow-on variants regardless of sender domain rotation
