## Why
- Telecommunications partner systems contain highly sensitive subscriber data including phone numbers, account details, and authentication information that directly enables SIM-swapping attacks
- Successful compromise of these systems allows threat actors to bypass multi-factor authentication protections and gain control of high-value cryptocurrency exchange accounts, resulting in millions of dollars in theft
- This technique represents a critical detection gap as unauthorized access to telecom partner infrastructure often goes undetected until after SIM-swap attacks have been executed and funds stolen
- The Polish cybercrime group treated this as a regular income source, indicating this is a repeatable and scalable attack pattern that other organized crime groups may adopt
