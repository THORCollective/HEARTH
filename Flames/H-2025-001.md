# H-2025-001

Threat actors are using the open-source tunneling utility Chisel to create SOCKS proxies on compromised machines in order to bypass network controls such as firewalls and hide their network communications when moving laterally within victim financial organizations in Africa.

## Why
- Chisel allows the attackers to tunnel network traffic through compromised hosts, concealing the true source and destination of their communications. This makes it much harder to detect and track the adversary's movement.
- By deploying Chisel as a SOCKS proxy on multiple machines, the threat actors can pivot and access different network segments that would otherwise be inaccessible. This expands their access and enables them to reach their objectives.
- The use of Chisel is part of a broader campaign by an initial access broker targeting financial institutions across Africa. Detecting this TTP can reveal affected organizations and lead to uncovering the full scope of this threat actor's operations.
