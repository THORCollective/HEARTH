# H-2025-001

Our threat intelligence team has recently identified a new campaign by a threat group targeting financial institutions. The group is known for its sophisticated tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs). In this campaign, the threat actors are using a combination of spear-phishing emails and malicious Microsoft Office documents to gain initial access to the target systems. Once inside, they are using a custom PowerShell-based backdoor to maintain persistence and perform lateral movement within the network. One of the unique TTPs observed in this campaign is the use of the "PowerShell Empire" post-exploitation framework to execute a series of commands that modify the Windows Registry to disable Windows Defender and other security features. This allows the threat actors to operate undetected and carry out their objectives, which include exfiltrating sensitive financial data.

## Why
- Detecting this behavior is crucial as it allows the threat actors to disable security features, making the systems vulnerable to further exploitation and data exfiltration.
- If successful, this tactic can lead to significant financial loss and reputational damage for the targeted institutions.
- This behavior is linked to a larger campaign targeting financial institutions, indicating a high level of sophistication and potential for widespread impact.
