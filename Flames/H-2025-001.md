# H-2025-001

Threat actors are using the open-source Chisel utility to create tunnels from infected client machines to attacker-controlled servers, enabling the adversaries to bypass network security controls and remotely access other systems on the victim's network.

## Why
- Chisel tunnels allow the threat actors to stealthily pivot from the initial compromised system to other machines, expanding their access and control over the victim's environment. This enables them to achieve their objectives of establishing footholds for sale as an initial access broker.
- By tunneling C2 traffic and proxying connections through Chisel, the attackers can evade network monitoring and security controls like firewalls that may otherwise detect and block their activity. This increases dwell time.
- The use of Chisel has been observed in conjunction with other open source tools like PoshC2 as part of a larger playbook employed by the CL-CRI-1014 cluster to compromise financial institutions across Africa. Detecting this TTP can lead to uncovering the full scope of this threat actor's campaigns.
