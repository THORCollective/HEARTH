# H-2025-001

Threat actors are using the GHOSTPULSE loader to deploy the ARECHCLIENT2 (SectopRAT) infostealer via an intermediate .NET loader, gaining remote control and extensive access to credentials, financial data, and system information on compromised Windows systems.

## Why
- The GHOSTPULSE loader has been observed in recent campaigns delivering the ARECHCLIENT2 infostealer, which has seen a considerable surge in malicious activity recently. The loader utilizes DLL sideloading and reflective loading to evade detection and deliver its payload.
- Success in this technique provides threat actors with remote control capabilities and extensive access to sensitive user and system data, including credentials, financial data, and extensive system profiling. This can lead to significant financial loss and operational disruption for the victim.
- The use of this technique is indicative of a multi-stage attack, with the potential for further malicious activity beyond the initial infostealer deployment. Detecting this behavior can provide valuable insight into ongoing campaigns and aid in mitigation efforts.
