# H-2025-001

Threat actors are using the open-source tool Chisel to create tunnels that bypass network controls like firewalls and proxy C2 traffic to infected machines, in order to maintain covert communication channels.

## Why
- Tunneling enables adversaries to bypass perimeter defenses and access restricted network segments. 
- Chisel tunnels allow threat actors to hide their true origin and make their C2 traffic appear to originate from the victim network.
- This activity is part of a larger campaign targeting financial institutions across Africa, likely to establish footholds and sell access to other criminals.
