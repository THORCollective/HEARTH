# H-2025-001

Threat actors are using the ClickFix social engineering technique to trick users into copying and pasting malicious PowerShell commands, which results in the execution of the GHOSTPULSE loader. This loader then deploys an intermediate .NET loader, ultimately delivering the ARECHCLIENT2 payload directly into memory. The immediate tactical goal of this technique is to gain initial access to the system, bypass traditional perimeter defenses, and establish a foothold for further malicious activities.

## Why
- Detecting the use of the ClickFix technique is crucial as it is a highly effective and prevalent initial access method that bypasses many traditional perimeter defenses.
- Successful execution of this technique allows threat actors to gain a foothold in the system, from which they can deploy further malicious activities, including data theft and remote control of the infected system.
- This technique has been linked to a larger campaign involving the GHOSTPULSE loader and the ARECHCLIENT2 payload, indicating a well-coordinated and potentially widespread threat.
