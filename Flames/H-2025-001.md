Adversaries are using OAuth consent phishing emails to trick users into granting malicious applications persistent access to their cloud accounts and sensitive data.

## Why
- OAuth consent phishing enables adversaries to gain long-term, stealthy access to cloud accounts and data without needing to compromise credentials directly. The malicious OAuth grants serve as an alternate, "backdoor" credential.
- Once granted, the attacker maintains access via OAuth tokens even if the victim changes their password or signs out. This allows persistent compromise of the account.
- Malicious OAuth apps often request broad permissions (e.g. Mail.ReadWrite, Files.ReadWrite.All) which give attackers extensive access to exfiltrate sensitive data from cloud mailboxes, drives, and other services.
- This technique has been used by multiple threat actors for espionage and data theft, including the DAMASCUS APT group.
