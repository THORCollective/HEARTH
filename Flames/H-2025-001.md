# H-2025-001

Threat actors are executing a combination of 'wmic' and 'shadowcopy' commands to create Volume Shadow Copies of data, facilitating data theft and potential encryption for ransomware attacks on critical Windows-based servers.

## Why
- Detecting this behavior is crucial as it can lead to substantial data theft. The creation of Volume Shadow Copies can also be a precursor to ransomware attacks, where data is encrypted and held hostage.
- If successful, adversaries could steal sensitive data and cause significant disruption to business operations, especially if the data is subsequently encrypted.
- It has been observed as part of larger campaigns where threat actors use multiple techniques to evade detection, steal data, and deploy ransomware.
