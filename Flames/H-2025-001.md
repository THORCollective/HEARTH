# H-2025-001

Our threat intelligence team has identified a new campaign by an advanced persistent threat (APT) group. The group is known for its sophisticated attacks on high-value targets, primarily in the financial sector. In this campaign, the APT group is using a new variant of a remote access trojan (RAT) that leverages the Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI) for persistence. The RAT is delivered via spear-phishing emails with malicious attachments. Once the RAT is installed, it uses WMI to create a permanent event subscription, which executes a PowerShell command whenever the system is booted. This ensures that the RAT maintains persistence on the infected system, even if the original binary is detected and removed.

---

Hypothesis:

Threat actors are leveraging Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI) to create a permanent event subscription, which executes a PowerShell command at system boot, to ensure their remote access trojan (RAT) maintains persistence on infected systems in the financial sector.

## Why
- Detecting this behavior is crucial as it allows threat actors to maintain a foothold in the targeted systems, enabling them to carry out further malicious activities.
- If successful, this technique can lead to significant data breaches and financial losses, especially considering the high-value targets in the financial sector.
- This behavior is linked to a larger campaign by an advanced persistent threat (APT) group, indicating a high level of sophistication and potential impact.
