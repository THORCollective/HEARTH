# H-2025-001

CTI Report:

Our threat intelligence sources have identified a new campaign by a sophisticated threat group. The group is known for its stealthy tactics and advanced techniques. In this campaign, they are using a novel method to bypass User Account Control (UAC) on Windows systems. They are leveraging the Windows Event Viewer (eventvwr.exe) to bypass UAC and execute their malicious payloads with high privileges. This technique is particularly concerning as it does not require any third-party tools or complex scripts, making it difficult to detect.

---

Hypothesis:

Threat actors are exploiting the auto-elevation feature of Windows Event Viewer (eventvwr.exe) to bypass User Account Control (UAC) and execute their malicious payloads with high privileges on targeted Windows systems.

## Why
- Detecting this behavior is crucial as it allows threat actors to escalate their privileges and execute malicious payloads with high privileges, potentially leading to full system compromise.
- The success of this technique can lead to significant damage, including data exfiltration, lateral movement, and persistence within the network.
- This technique has been linked to sophisticated threat groups, indicating a high level of threat and potential association with larger, coordinated campaigns.
