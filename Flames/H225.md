# H225

Sekoia's analysis of Gamaredon's (FSB-linked) **GammaSteel** infostealer documented a removable-media collection capability wired to a real-time trigger: the PowerShell orchestrator registers a WMI event subscription that fires the instant a USB drive is attached, then harvests documents and archives into a hidden, MachineGuid-named staging folder. The durable hunting angle is the pairing — a `Win32_LogicalDisk DriveType=2` WMI watcher created by PowerShell, followed by extension-targeted document collection from the newly mounted drive.

## Why

- **The WMI removable-drive subscription is a distinctive, verbatim artifact.** A `__InstanceCreationEvent` filter on `Win32_LogicalDisk` with `DriveType = 2` created by `powershell.exe` is rare outside of purpose-built USB-monitoring malware and is captured directly by 4104 and WMI-Activity ETW.
- **Discovery + collection together beat either alone.** Removable-drive discovery is benign on its own and document reads are benign on their own; the malicious signal is the subscription arming a document-harvest burst into a hidden GUID-named folder within seconds of a USB attach.
- **Air-gapped and USB-borne intel theft is Gamaredon's stock in trade.** This loop is how documents leave hosts that never touch the internet directly — catching the subscription gives defenders the earliest signal, before staging and exfil.
- **Cheap telemetry, high leverage.** Script-block logging plus WMI-Activity ETW already exist in most Windows estates; no new sensor is needed to hunt the subscription and the collection burst.
